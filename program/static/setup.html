How setup works — two passes

Pass 1

Walk & map

Build your approved bottle map — names, stations, sizes. No levels yet. We stay humble when speech is messy; you fix what we got wrong.

Pass 2

Count & reconcile

Count levels against that map. If everything lines up, we lock your baseline. If not, we hand you a line-by-line comparison and invite you to fix and re-upload.

Every input is a proposal. Reconciliation is the conversation until map and count agree.

  • Pass 1: Walk every bar by voice — say Bar 1, Bar 2, Bar 3 as you go
  • Stations inside each bar: Well one, Well two, Back bar, Beer cooler, Wine cooler…
  • Reconcile, review, and approve the map contract
  • Pass 2: First live count — lock baseline or fix gaps

Better voice notes = less cleanup later

Built-in phone dictation (iPhone Notes, Android keyboard) works, but it garbles brand names, bottle sizes, and fast bar-room speech — especially on a ladder with music in the background.

Wispr Flow (iPhone/Mac) is built for this: hold a button, talk naturally, export clean text. Strong alternatives: VoiceInk (Mac, open source), Whisperboard or TypeWhisper (iPhone, on-device). Any of these beat raw phone mic for a full back-bar walk.

No special app required — but if your first upload looks rough, try one of the above before your next bar.

This program runs on your machine — no AI required

Everything you installed is self-contained. Voice walks, counts, reconciliation, spreadsheets, PAR levels, and weekly Process all run locally on this laptop. Upload your notes, fix what we got wrong, and lock your inventory — no subscription, no cloud, no API key needed.

Optional add-on

AI connection — only if you want it

The core system does not use AI. Connect a key only if you want help reading invoice photos from your phone (Southern Glazer’s, Breakthru, etc.) instead of typing line items yourself. Coming soon: wine-cooler video walks parsed from your phone.

You do not need a paid AI subscription for the program itself. Many providers (Claude, ChatGPT, Grok, and others) include API access with plans you may already have — we’re a working project and will add more free-friendly options as they emerge. Optional email updates coming for weekly release notes.

  • Without AI: paste invoice text, type numbers in spreadsheets, voice-walk and count as usual
  • With AI: snap a photo of a paper invoice → line items land in All inputs
  • Later: add or remove your key anytime under Settings → Optional AI

What happens when you parse

Real bar walks are messy — and that's expected. Fast speech, ladder work, and phone transcription turn "Herradura Reposado 1L" into something unrecognizable. The first pass is a draft placement, not a perfect inventory list.

Bottle sizes default to 750ml until you correct them. Brand names may land on the wrong station or split into fragments. You'll fix names, sizes, and categories in the next steps — and again on the road every cycle. Upload is the starting line, not the finish.

Step 6 · Pass 2 — Count

First Live Inventory Count

Pass 2

What we need from you: Station by station — each bottle on your approved map with its level (full or tenths).

What happens next: We compare your count to the walk map. Golden match → lock baseline. Gaps → we hand back a comparison; fix the map or re-upload until it lines up.

This count becomes your baseline for every future cycle. We block a bad baseline — reconcile surprises before you finish.

Before you count

  • Count full bottles and open bottle levels (tenths) against your approved map — station by station.
  • Liter bottles and 750ml bottles look alike on the shelf. Double-check size before you lock a number.
  • Call out partials honestly — a bad baseline poisons every future cycle.
  • One station at a time. Finish a section before you jump to the next.
  • When every station is entered, mark the count complete to open home base.

How to submit your count

Easiest option: Paste below, then click Process — or upload a file from your phone. Speak into iPhone Notes, Google Keep, Samsung Notes, or any app that exports .txt or .md — then transfer the file to this computer and upload.

Or paste your verbal count directly in the box. Call out the station, then each bottle with its level. Full bottles as one or 2 (or Spanish uno / lleno). Open bottles as tenths: point five, 0.5, half, or Spanish medio / vacío.

"Well one. Tito's one. Ketel One point five. Tanqueray one. Bacardi zero. Row two. Espolon one. Evan Williams point three. Back bar top shelf. Patron one. Don Julio point eight…"
